--- layout: default title: Promotion Endorsements ---

One signal of career progression for an engineer is that their impact is positively felt by an ever increasing number of people. Furthermore, it's important to fairly assess people from across the organization, particularly so in more senior roles where they would be considered role models.

With fairness and impact in mind, managers should use the table below as a guide in who endoresments should be collected from. These endorsements should be specific, contain examples and not passive/generic statements such as "I have no problem with this". That requirement becomes critical for senior promotions and up.

A possible outcome of gathering endorsements could be areas for improvement, that may mean a promotion is delayed while the candidate is coached. This should not be seen as a negative outcome, but beneficial in personal development or challenging perceptions. If somebody is well managed, the feedback coming out of this process should not be a surprise. It's very important to have an expectation that the endorsement process is not a formality.

Promotion Step Endorsements required from...
From Junior Engineer to Engineer
  1. Current manager & HR Business Partner
  2. Senior Engineer or above
  3. Director of Engineering
From Engineer to Senior Engineer
  1. Current manager & HR Business Partner
  2. Engineering Manager from another team
  3. Lead Engineer or above
  4. Director of Engineering
From Senior Engineer to Lead Engineer
  1. Current manager & HR Business Partner
  2. Engineering Manager from another team
  3. Two Directors of Engineering
  4. Principal Engineer or above
  5. Chief Technology Officer
From Lead Engineer to Principal Engineer
  1. Current manager & HR Business Partner
  2. Two Directors of Engineering
  3. Distinguished Engineer
  4. Chief Technology Officer
  5. Group Chief Technology Officer
From Principal Engineer to Distinguished Engineer
  1. Current manager & HR Business Partner
  2. All Engineering Directors
  3. Chief Technology Officer
  4. Group Chief Technology Officer
